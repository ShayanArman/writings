The Opportunity Cost they face is so much higher, and their skills with Technology (a leverage, scaling machine), is more acute, that they *can produce insane results, because they *have to.

A startup has GOT to use technology as leverage.

An individual can quit their job, and attempt a restaurant or a cafe. And their goal as a proprietor will be to spend a few years until they can pay their salary.

Its very singular, consistent, and even linear.

They can hope to pay their salary, or % more.

Usually a person opening a cafe doesn’t think:

“Im gonna be a Billionaire from this.”

But Startups are different. They use technology as Leverage, increasing their output as individuals.

They also *usually sell products that can Scale.

A cafe can scale to a few thousand coffees served a day. Same with a restaurant. Franchising takes lots of time.

But a factory can increase output simply by increasing the order.

The limiting factor usually is demand not production.
