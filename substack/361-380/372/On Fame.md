On Fame

Of course. Here’s what you said: You said, “I’ve been telling a lot of you that I’m famous, that I feel famous, and that a lot of people have actually told me that I’m famous. And now I can finally tell you the actual truth, the whole truth. What I realized was, I actually am famous. I truly feel famous. And what that means is, I feel like I get treated like I’m famous because of my personality and the way that I treat others in the world. It obviously doesn’t mean that everybody knows me, but it means that the feeling in my heart is one of fame. It means that the way I treat people, everybody I meet, with such a nonchalant attitude because I meet so many people in my life, is not so dissimilar to someone who’s actually famous and the way that they interact with other people. And only people who interacted with this many people and who are so at ease with speaking to complete and utter strangers, you know this feeling. I would actually argue that many people who are famous, they even don’t get to this level. And the way that you are treated, the way that you treat others, the way that you speak to them, the way that you can speak to strangers at whim, that is what true fame feels like and how they treat you back and how they want to give you more just because of the weight of your personality.”
