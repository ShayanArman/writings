Where is it in the body? Where is happiness?

It is also a production. So then the question should be what does it take to create happiness?

The idea that someone is a happy person is ridiculous. If his cat dies in front of him, what man isn’t sad?

The causes of the production of happiness in the body are multifold.

A lack of production of pain helps. A full belly helps. And security and safety help. These things aid in the production of happiness. The nervous system can produce happiness.

Why is it that we must have safety to produce happiness? Because the Brain is a state machine. And two states seldom cannot be occurring at the same time. Priority is given to one produced state over another. If you’re getting chased by a robber, the brain chooses fear or aggression. Not relaxed let’s coombaya. It is exlusivity (happy but not sad, scared but not loving), plus hierarchical states (fear trumps happy), (love trumps fear).

Or better put. Each moment dictates a response. A state to represent the moment. And the moment is exclusive in that one state is chosen at any given time.

Is the brain capable of such exclusivity in states. Why yes. It’s noise cancelling ability is absolute unparalleled. Think “the cat went to his right and found a dog and mistook it for a mouse” that voice was singular and exclusive. No other words were played in your mind at the point of thought.

Exclusivity seems to be the key to the universe and the mind. No two particles can occupy the same space. And no two thoughts may be had at the same moment. One will win out based on the strength of the signal.
