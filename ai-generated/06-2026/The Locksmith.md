The Locksmith
and doors we call enemies
I met a locksmith at 2:14 in the morning.
Nobody meets a locksmith then because life is going well.
The door looked at me.
I looked at the door.
It said nothing, which somehow made it more annoying.
He arrived, put a little stick in the lock, and 12 seconds later I was home.
"Thats it?" I asked.
"No," he said, "thats knowing which 12 seconds."
Most problems are doors.
From outside they become theology.
From inside they are hinges and a handle.
A lock is a question; a key is an answer with teeth.
Dumbo.
